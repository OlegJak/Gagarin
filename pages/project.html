Silkscreen course. Erasmus+ project "Samm-sammult"

Finally, our very old dream came true, and at the end of January of this year, as part of the Erasmus + Samm-Summult project, we got into a silk-screen printing course. Soon we will bring from Poland to Narva to our visitors this wonderful technique of drawing drawings on almost any surface.

The training covered the full production cycle from scratch. Screen printing technology is the sequential printing of an image on various surfaces. Training covered all processes:

  • selection and production of sketches by computer programs, printing of sketches on film
  • research of processes and chemical components
  • preparation of special frames for printing
  • application of emulsion and other chemical components
  • emulsion lighting, frame washing
  • preparing the workspace for printing
  • printing on paper and fabric
  • finishing and confirmation of impressions

Stained glass painting, lead stained glass - our courses for the Erasmus+ project "Step by step"

Recently, our teachers returned from amazing courses from Italy, from the city of Bolsena. Here, in the workshop of two wonderful masters, Diego and Andrea, we learned the secrets of classical stained glass painting using the grisaille technique, and also learned how to make lead stained glass windows. It was a fantastic week filled with discoveries, emotions and impressions. The training took place within the framework of our large Samm-Summult project under the Erasmus + program.

During the 7-day course, our teachers fully developed their individual project - from choosing a sketch to making a finished product, got acquainted with the ancient technology of glass painting, traditional glass paints, different types of paints, how they can be mixed and used to create a shadow effect, as well as with paints that create effects of transparency and opacity.

We learned how to properly mix pigments and paint parts of glass with transparent enamels. We studied the different types of glass on the market and which ones are best suited for painting glass, and received recommendations on what temperatures to use for various stains.

Micromosaic course in Italy. Erasmus+ project "Summ-Summult"

We work for our students and guests during the academic year, and we try to use the summer as much as possible for teaching our own. We recently completed the first stage of a very important project for us - a 5-day full-fledged course on the mosaic "Summult" under the Erasmus + program.

As part of this course, we study technologies unique to Narva (and maybe Estonia) in order to introduce them to the residents of our city later. The first were classical ancient Roman mosaics and micromosaics using natural stone and smalt. We will definitely structure the impressions of this trip, and in the near future, at our pilot master classes, we will introduce visitors to this technology and tell how warmly and at the same time demandingly our Italian master teachers met us. And ahead of us are waiting for new knowledge, new acquaintances, new partners and new goals.
